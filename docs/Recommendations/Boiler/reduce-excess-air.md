# Reduce Excess Air in Boiler Flue Gas
Reducing excess air in boiler flue gas improves combustion efficiency, lowers fuel consumption, and reduces stack losses. This measure is best applied after a combustion analysis confirms that the boiler is operating with more air than required for safe, complete combustion.
 
**ARC Code(s):** 2.2411 (REDUCE EXCESS AIR IN BOILER FLUE GAS)
 
## Savings Calculation
 
Combustion efficiency is a key driver of boiler fuel use. If the boiler output remains constant, improving combustion efficiency reduces the fuel required to deliver that same heat.
 
Let:

- $\eta_{\text{baseline}}$ = measured combustion efficiency before tuning (fraction)
- $\eta_{\text{optimized}}$ = target combustion efficiency after reducing excess air (fraction)
- $Q_{\text{fuel}}$ = actual annual boiler fuel input (MMBtu/yr)
 
The estimated annual fuel savings are:

$$
\Delta \text{MMBtu} = Q_{\text{fuel}} \times \left(1 - \frac{\eta_{\text{baseline}}}{\eta_{\text{optimized}}}\right)
$$

where:

- $\Delta \text{MMBtu}$ = annual fuel savings (MMBtu/yr)
- $Q_{\text{fuel}}$ = actual annual boiler fuel input (MMBtu/yr)
- $\eta_{\text{baseline}}$ = baseline combustion efficiency (fraction)
- $\eta_{\text{optimized}}$ = combustion efficiency after excess air reduction (fraction)
 
Annual cost savings are then:

$$
\text{Annual Savings} = \Delta \text{MMBtu} \times R_{\text{fuel}}
$$

where:

- $R_{\text{fuel}}$ = delivered fuel price ($/MMBtu)
 
### Target combustion conditions
 
Aim for the lowest safe excess air or O₂ levels for the fuel type. Typical targets are:

- Natural gas: 3–5% O₂
- LPG or propane: 2–4% O₂
- Oil: 0.5–1.5% O₂
 
Maintain combustion safety by verifying flame stability, CO, and stack temperature after tuning.
 
## Anticipated Costs
 
This recommendation is primarily a maintenance and tuning measure. Equipment costs are minimal if the facility already has combustion analysis instrumentation.
 
**Labor:** Estimate 2–4 hours of qualified boiler technician time per boiler for combustion tuning, burner adjustment, and verification.
 
## Report Requirements
 
- Document the baseline and post-tuning combustion analyzer results, including O₂, CO, stack temperature, and excess air if available.
- Include the baseline and optimized combustion efficiencies used in the savings calculation.
- State the fuel type, fuel cost, and annual fuel input used to calculate $\Delta \text{MMBtu}$.
- Note any combustion safety limits or manufacturer instructions that influenced the target operating point.
