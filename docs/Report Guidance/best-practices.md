# Best Practices

The Best Practices section is found under **Report Sections** → **Best-Practices.tex** in the LaTeX sidebar. It presents a curated list of the energy efficiency and manufacturing practices already in place at the facility, giving the client credit for what they are doing well before the report moves into recommendations.

## What to Do

The LaTeX file contains the full list of possible best practices as pre-written bullet points. **Do not write new entries** — simply delete the lines that do not apply to this facility. What remains should reflect only what was actually observed or confirmed.

The list to select from is:

- LED lighting system has been installed in part of the facility.
- Proper rate schedules for utilities.
- Roof renovations was recently performed.
- Refrigerated dryers are installed after each compressor.
- Noise level awareness for loud areas and signs posted requiring hearing protection.
- Statistical quality control management system overview of each machine.
- BMS system managing all major equipment in the facility.
- Backup generators for safety/emergency.
- VFDs installed for many motor-driven equipment (pumps, blower fans, etc.).
- Notched motor belts are used for belt-driven equipment (e.g., ventilation fans).
- Continued commitment to energy efficiency for retrofitting and seeking efficiency measures to reduce energy expenditures.
- Occupancy sensors installed in low-traffic areas to reduce unnecessary lighting.
- Programmable thermostats to optimize HVAC scheduling during occupied and unoccupied periods.
- High-efficiency motors (NEMA Premium or IE3/IE4) used for critical equipment.
- Power factor correction capacitors installed to minimize reactive power charges.
- Compressed air leak detection program with regular audits to identify and repair leaks.
- Receiver tanks properly sized to reduce compressor cycling and short-loading.
- Insulated steam and hot water pipes to minimize thermal losses in distribution systems.
- Steam trap maintenance program with regular inspections and replacements.
- Economizer controls on boilers to preheat feedwater using flue gas heat recovery.
- Destratification fans installed in high-bay areas to recirculate warm air during heating season.
- High-speed doors or strip curtains at loading docks to reduce infiltration losses.
- Demand-controlled ventilation adjusting outside air intake based on occupancy or CO₂ levels.
- Premium air filters with appropriate MERV ratings balanced for air quality and pressure drop.
- Reflective or cool roofing materials to reduce solar heat gain during cooling season.
- Preventive maintenance program with scheduled equipment inspections and servicing.
- Energy submetering installed on major systems to track consumption and identify anomalies.
- Waste heat recovery from compressors, ovens, or process equipment for space or water heating.
- Natural daylighting through skylights or clerestory windows in production areas.
- Automatic shutoff controls for equipment during breaks, shift changes, or weekends.
- Condensate return system to recycle hot condensate back to the boiler feedwater tank.
- Energy awareness training provided to employees on conservation practices.

## How to Decide What to Keep

Base your selections on information gathered from three sources, in order of reliability:

1. **On-site assessment** — what you directly observed during the facility walkthrough
2. **Pre-assessment meetings** — details shared by the facility contact beforehand
3. **Any supplemental information** obtained from the client (data sheets, utility bills, facility documentation, etc.)

    If you are unsure whether a practice applies — for example, you recall seeing something on the walkthrough but aren't certain — consult the **Student Lead** or other students who attended the assessment before including or removing that item. It is better to ask than to misrepresent what the facility is doing.

    Only include practices that were **confirmed** for this facility. Do not leave in items simply because they seem plausible or common for the industry. Every bullet that remains is an implicit claim that this practice is in place.
